Word: Love — ʾahav (אָהַב)

Define

  • Hebrew: ʾahav (אָהַב) — to love, cherish, have affection for, desire, choose, and remain loyal in covenant relationship. In the Hebrew mindset, love is demonstrated by faithful action rather than merely emotion. It involves devotion, commitment, and obedience (Deuteronomy 6:5; 10:12; 11:1; 30:6).
  • Greek: agapaō (ἀγαπάω) / agapē (ἀγάπη) — to love with purposeful, self-giving devotion; love expressed by seeking another's good. The Septuagint (LXX) most commonly translates ʾahav with agapaō.

Related Words

  • ahavah (אַהֲבָה) — love, affection (noun) (Song of Solomon 2:4; Jeremiah 2:2)
  • dod (דּוֹד) — beloved, lover (Song of Solomon 1:2)
  • chesed (חֶסֶד) — steadfast love, covenant faithfulness, lovingkindness (Exodus 34:6)
  • racham (רָחַם) — to have compassion, show tender mercy (Deuteronomy 13:17)
  • agapaō (ἀγαπάω) — to love (Greek)
  • phileō (φιλέω) — to love as a friend, to have affection (Greek)

Additional Information

  • The first occurrence of ʾahav is God's command concerning Abraham's "son, your only son, whom you love" (Genesis 22:2), connecting love with sacrifice and trust.
  • Israel is commanded to love YHWH with all heart, soul, and might (Deuteronomy 6:5).
  • Love for God is repeatedly linked with keeping His commandments (Deuteronomy 11:1, 30:16).
  • YHWH's love for Israel is rooted in His covenant choice, not Israel's greatness (Deuteronomy 7:7–8; 10:15).
  • Love extends beyond God to one's neighbor (Leviticus 19:18) and even the stranger (Leviticus 19:34; Deuteronomy 10:19).
  • In the New Testament, Yeshua identifies love for God and neighbor as summing up the Torah and the Prophets (Matthew 22:37–40), quoting Deuteronomy 6:5 and Leviticus 19:18.

Connections

  • Deuteronomy 6:5 — Love YHWH with all your heart, soul, and might.
  • Deuteronomy 10:12 — Love is part of what YHWH requires of Israel.
  • Deuteronomy 10:19 — Love the sojourner because Israel were sojourners in Egypt.
  • Deuteronomy 11:1 — Love expressed through keeping His charge, statutes, judgments, and commandments.
  • Joshua 22:5 — Love YHWH by walking in His ways and serving Him.
  • 1 Samuel 15:22 — Obedience is valued above sacrifice.
  • Hosea 6:6 — YHWH desires steadfast love (chesed) rather than sacrifice.
  • John 14:15 — "If you love me, keep my commandments."
  • John 14:21 — Loving Yeshua is demonstrated by keeping His commandments.
  • 1 John 5:2–3 — The love of God is keeping His commandments, and His commandments are not burdensome.
