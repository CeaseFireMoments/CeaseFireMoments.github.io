Sabbath

##GateWisdom ##AppointedTime ##Sabbath #S

Define

  • Hebrew: (shabbat) root verb (shabat, H7673) meaning "to cease, desist, rest." SabbathS: shabbtay ()
  • Greek: (sabbaton) plural (sabbata); derived from Hebrew shabbat, meaning "Sabbath" or "week."

Related words

  • Hebrew: (shabbaton, H7677) a solemn rest or sabbath observance (e.g., Exod 16:23; Lev 23:3).
  • Greek: (prosabbaton, G4315) the day before the Sabbath (Mark 15:42).

Additional Info

  • Instituted at creation as a day of rest after six days of work (Gen 2:2-3; Exod 20:11).
  • It is the seventh day of the week, commanded as a perpetual covenant sign between God and His people (Israelites) (Exod 20:8-11; 31:16-17; Deut 5:12-15).
  • Violating it by working was punishable by death (Exod 31:14-15; 35:2; Num 15:32-36).
  • It serves as a holy convocation for rest and worship (Lev 23:3; Isa 58:13-14).
  • In the New Testament, Jesus declares Himself Lord of the Sabbath (Matt 12:8; Mark 2:28; Luke 6:5)
  • Jesus and the Seventh Day Rest

Connections

  • Fourth commandment in the Decalogue (Exod 20:8-11; Deut 5:12-15).
  • Prophetic restoration (Isa 56:2-6; 66:23; Ezek 44:24; 46:1-3).
