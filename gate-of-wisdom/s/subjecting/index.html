CeaseFire Moments

Define

  • Hebrew: kana (כָּנַע) — to humble oneself, to submit, to be brought low
  • Greek: hypotassō (ὑποτάσσω) — to arrange under, to subordinate, to subject oneself voluntarily

Related words

  • hypo (ὑπό) — under
  • tassō (τάσσω) — to arrange, to appoint, to order
  • hypotage (ὑποταγή) — submission, subjection
  • anypotaktos (ἀνυπότακτος) — unruly, not subject, insubordinate
  • kana (כָּנַע) — to subdue, humble
  • shama (שָׁמַע) — to hear, to obey

Additional Info

  • The verb form in Ephesians 5:21 is the reflexive/middle voice — hypotassomenoi (ὑποτασσόμενοι) — meaning the action is voluntary and self-initiated, not imposed
  • Ephesians 5:21 applies it mutually: "submitting to one another"
  • The same word is used of Jesus submitting to the Father (1 Corinthians 15:28) and of Jesus submitting to his parents (Luke 2:51)

Connections

  • Ephesians 5:21 — mutual submission of all believers
  • Luke 2:51 — Jesus hypotassō to Mary and Joseph
  • Romans 13:1 — submitting to governing authorities
  • James 4:7 — submit to God
  • 1 Samuel 7:13 — Philistines kana before Israel
  • Psalm 18:47 — peoples kana under David
  • Hierarchical Relationships Addressed by God or Jesus
  • Equality Addressed by God or Jesus
