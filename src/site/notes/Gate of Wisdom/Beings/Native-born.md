# Define

- Hebrew: ezrach (אֶזְרָח) — one who springs up naturally from the soil; a full member of the covenant community by birth and inheritance. The image is agricultural — like a plant that has grown from its native ground, rooted and belonging. Carries the sense of established standing, land-rights, and full legal protection within the community.
- Greek: autochthon (αὐτόχθων) — one from the land itself; indigenous. The LXX rendering reinforces the rootedness of the term — sprung from the very earth of the place.

# Related Words

- yalid (יְלִיד) — one born in the house; used of household-born servants (Genesis 17:12-13) — born within but not necessarily of full standing
- ben (בֵּן) — son; membership by descent

# Additional Information

- The pairing of ezrach and ger in v.34 is deliberate — the native-born is the standard of belonging, and the command extends that same standard to the sojourner: _"The stranger who lives as a foreigner with you shall be to you as the native-born among you"_ (Leviticus 19:34, WEB)
- Numbers 9:14 — the same law of Passover applies to both ger and ezrach: _"You shall have one statute, both for the stranger and for him who is born in the land."_ (WEB)
- Numbers 15:15-16 — _"One statute shall be for you of the assembly and for the stranger who lives as a foreigner with you... as you are, so shall the foreigner be before Yahweh. One law and one ordinance shall be for you and for the stranger who lives as a foreigner with you."_ (WEB)

# Connections

- Leviticus 24:22 — _"You shall have one kind of law for the foreigner as for the native-born."_ (WEB)
- Numbers 9:14; 15:15-16, 29 — equal standing before the law for ger and ezrach
- Exodus 12:48-49 — the circumcised sojourner may keep Passover and is as the ezrach
- Ezekiel 47:22 — in the vision of the restored land, the ger is granted inheritance among the tribes — _"they shall be to you as the native-born among the children of Israel."_ (WEB)

# Personal Notes

[[Sacred Truces/Foreigner, Sojourner, Stranger Among You\|Foreigner, Sojourner, Stranger Among You]]
- In English this appears as “native-born”, “homeborn,” and “native of the land”. Someone born into the people of Israel.
- There were two ways to be deemed native-born:
	- Through the father’s line. So if the father was descendant from Jacob/Israel, he was a native born.
	- Two former Gers have a child. Their children after that would be considered full Israelite by birth. Except Priesthood because that came from the line of Aaron.
- These people receive full inheritance of the land, full participation in rituals and feasts. If from Aaron’s line they could participate in serving in the Tabernacle/Temple. Could become King. They are a part of the covenant through birth and have automatic inclusion in the inheritance promised. (Exod 12, Deut 17, Lev 24)
