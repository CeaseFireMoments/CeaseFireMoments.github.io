# Define
- Hebrew: sherets (שֶׁרֶץ)  
	- swarming thing, creeping thing, swarm  
- Greek: herpeton (ἑρπετόν)  
	- creeping thing, reptile  

# Related words
- sharats (שָׁרַץ) — to swarm, to teem, to creep  
- remes (רֶמֶשׂ) — creeping thing, moving creature  

# Additional Info
- Leviticus 11:29 — “These also shall be unclean unto you among the creeping things that creep upon the earth” (sherets ha-shoretz)  
- Leviticus 11:41 — “Every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth” (kol-ha-sherets ha-shoretz)  
- Leviticus 11:20 — winged creeping things (sherets ha-`oph)

Meaning: The term remes refers to creatures that "creep," "crawl," or move close to the ground. It typically includes small animals, insects, reptiles, or other creatures that scurry or slither, such as lizards, mice, or bugs. In some contexts, it may broadly encompass animals that move in a particular manner.

Genesis 9:3 (KJV): "Every moving thing (remes) that liveth shall be meat for you." //allowed as food// Here, remes is used to describe creatures that are part of the food allowance given to Noah and his descendants.

In Genesis 9:2, remes appears in the phrase "every creeping thing (remes) that is upon the earth," alongside beasts, fowl, and fish, indicating a subset of creatures under human dominion.

While remes does not appear directly in [Genesis 8](brain://UOcBnFDnElCRdgy71FsFLA/Genesis8?name=Genesis%208):21, the related verse Genesis 8:17 mentions "every creeping thing (remes) that creepeth upon the earth" among the creatures Noah is to release from the ark, setting the stage for the covenant in [Genesis 9](brain://E--cqDTu2Veo5XZvGOsBHQ/Genesis9?name=Genesis%209).

Broader Biblical Context: Remes is used in creation accounts (e.g., [Genesis 1](brain://Jwm-duKoHVWzCeWdxM2KVA/Genesis1?name=Genesis%201):24–25, where it denotes creatures distinct from cattle and beasts) and other passages (e.g., Leviticus 11:44–46, where it includes unclean creeping creatures).
