# Define
- Hebrew: toshav (תּוֹשָׁב) temporary resident, sojourner, settler, inhabitant. Often times appears as the Ger Toshav.   The lived AMONG the Israelites, inside the camp or town. Non-Israelite living in the land. Accepted basic moral standards. Not fully converted.
- Greek: paroikos (πάροικος) sojourner, resident alien, one dwelling beside

# Additional Info
- toshav often appears together with ger (ger vetoshav) to describe non-native residents living among Israel with limited rights.

# Personal Notes

[[Sacred Truces/Foreigner, Sojourner, Stranger Among You\|Foreigner, Sojourner, Stranger Among You]]
- **Toshav**
	- In English translations you might see “Non-Israelite living in the land,” “temporary resident”, “resident alien”, “foreigner”, “sojourner”. See passages such as Exodus 12:14, 45; Leviticus 25:6, 23. It is often pared with Ger to become “stranger and sojourner”. This is translated as alien, sojourner, foreigner, exile in the New Testament, see Acts 7:9, 1 Peter 2:11, Ephesians 2:19.
	- These people live WITHIN the land of Israel, but were less integrated. They can legally live and work in the community but have limited benefits. They might be a hired worker, tenant, or short-term settler. Their stay intent is more on a temporary basis. They accept the “moral” laws and reside under the Israelite authorities. They were a resident alien in more of an economic way.
	- A Toshav is an uncircumcised non-Israelite living in the land. Once circumcised, he becomes a native-born. The distinction between a Toshav and a Ger Toshav isn’t always easy to determine and are used interchangeably. The difference is a Toshav is more temporary where a Ger Toshav is more permanent.
	- The Roman Centurion would almost fit this category because he was living WITHIN the land of Israel. He asked Jesus for his servant to be healed. Jesus recognized his faith and healed his servant.
	- Using the WEB version of the bible, this usually appears as “stranger who lives as a foreigner”, “resident alien”, “foreigner who resides among you”. In others you might see “resident alien” or [[Gate of Wisdom/Beings/Sojourner\|Sojourner]]”. See passages such as Leviticus 19:33-34.
- **Ger Toshav**
	- The sojourner was someone lived AMONG the Israelites, inside the camp or town. They were a non-Israelite living in the land, pitching their tents among the tents of the Israelites. Their intent is to stay long term or permanently. Accepted basic “moral” standards. Not fully converted. They weren’t integrated into the community and weren’t necessarily interested in becoming a member of the community. Think of these people as being those in a country on a green card status. They have been given permission to live and work freely, legal protections, access to many services, but not full citizen.	
	- They agreed to follow the key laws in order to be allowed to live peacefully among the Israelites. But they did not fully convert to becoming an Israelite. They kept their own identity and didn’t swear allegiance to Israel. Later, this meant they would generally follow the Noahide laws.	
	- The Israelites were forbidden to mistreat or oppress these people. They could be given food and clothing as they were seen as vulnerable. This meant they were allowed to glean the fields, vineyards, and crops along with the native orphans and widows. The native-born would harvest their crops but were instructed not to do the corners and they couldn’t go back for a second time to get what was missed. THEN the sojourner, poor, orphans, and widows could go out and pick what was left. In addition, every third year people brought 10% of “everything in the land, whether grain or fruit because it belongs to the Lord” to be stored up. This was meant to provide for the Levites, the poor, orphans, widows, and sojourners. It was a community welfare system. They could also receive produce from the Sabbath year (Lev 25, Lev 27, Num 18)	
	- Some of the “mixed multitude” that came out of Egypt would have been in this category. The woman at the well is a pretty good example of this category.	
	- A Toshav is an uncircumcised non-Israelite living in the land. Once circumcised, he becomes a native-born
