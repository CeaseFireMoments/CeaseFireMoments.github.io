# Deuteronomy 27:16 — "Cursed"

## The Word

**arur** (אָרוּר)
## Hebrew

- Root: _arar_ (אָרַר)
- Form: qal passive participle — _arur_ is not a verb of action happening in the moment but a state of being declared over a person
- The participle form means the person is cursed — they enter and inhabit a condition of curse, not merely that a curse is spoken at them

## Meaning at Time of Writing

_Arar_ in the Hebrew mindset carries the sense of being bound, hemmed in, restricted — cut off from the flow of blessing. Where _barak_ (בָּרַךְ — blessing) opens, expands, and releases, _arar_ constricts and severs.

To be _arur_ is to be:

- Cut off from Yahweh's favor
- Removed from the covenant's protective covering
- Placed outside the sphere where life and flourishing operate

It is not primarily an emotional pronouncement — it is a covenantal and ontological status declared before Yahweh and witnessed by the community.

## Key Occurrences Establishing Its Weight

- Gen. 3:14 — the serpent is _arur_ above all creatures — severed from its former standing
- Gen. 3:17 — the ground is _arur_ because of Adam — creation itself enters a restricted, diminished state
- Gen. 4:11 — Cain is _arur_ from the ground — his severance is specifically from the source of his livelihood
- Gen. 9:25 — Canaan declared _arur_ — a generational covenantal status
- Num. 5:27 — the unfaithful wife becomes a curse (_arur_) among her people
- Deut. 27:15-26 — twelve _arur_ declarations, each confirmed by communal _amen_ — a covenant curse liturgy
- Deut. 28:16-19 — _arur_ applied to city, field, basket, offspring, land — totality of life affected
- Jer. 11:3 — _arur_ pronounced on the one who does not obey the covenant words
- Jer. 17:5 — _arur_ is the man who trusts in man and makes flesh his strength
