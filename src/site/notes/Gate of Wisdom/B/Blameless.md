Word: Blameless / Tamim — General

# Define

- **Hebrew**: tamim (תָּמִים) — whole, complete, without defect. From the root tam (תָּם) meaning completeness or integrity. The primary image is of something that is entirely whole — nothing missing, nothing divided, nothing defective. In the Hebrew mindset this is first a physical concept (an unblemished animal) that extends into the moral and covenantal — a person wholly oriented toward God with nothing held back and no divided allegiance.
- **Greek (LXX)**: teleios (τέλειος) — complete, perfect, having reached its intended end or fullness. The Greek carries a sense of maturity and completion of purpose rather than mere moral innocence.

# Related Words

- tam (תָּם) — the root; integrity, wholeness (Job 1:1; Psalm 37:37)
- tummah (תֻּמָּה) — integrity, uprightness (Proverbs 11:3)
- shalem (שָׁלֵם) — complete, at peace; from the same conceptual family as shalom (שָׁלוֹם) — wholeness expressed relationally
- amim (אָמִים) — without blemish, used specifically of sacrificial animals (Leviticus 22:21)

# Additional Information

- Used of sacrificial animals — the requirement that offerings be tamim (Leviticus 1:3; 22:21) establishes that the word first means physically whole and without defect before it carries moral weight
- [[Noah\|Noah]] — _"Noah was a righteous man, tamim among the people of his time"_ (Genesis 6:9, WEB)
- [[Gate of Wisdom/Beings/Human Beings/Abraham\|Abraham]] — _"Walk before me and be tamim"_ (Genesis 17:1, WEB) — God's direct charge; wholeness of walk before God
- Deuteronomy 18:13 — _"You shall be tamim with Yahweh your God"_ (WEB) — in context of the prohibition on divination; tamim here means wholly oriented toward Yahweh with no divided seeking
- Psalm 18:30 — _"As for God, his way is tamim"_ (WEB) — the word applied to God himself
- Psalm 119:1 — _"Blessed are those whose ways are tamim"_ (WEB)

# Connections

- Genesis 6:9 — Noah described as tamim
- Genesis 17:1 — God's charge to Abraham
- Leviticus 1:3; 22:21 — sacrificial standard of tamim
- Deuteronomy 18:13 — the command to Israel to be tamim before Yahweh
- Psalm 18:30 — God's own way described as tamim
- Matthew 5:48 — _"You therefore shall be teleios as your heavenly Father is teleios"_ (WEB) — Yeshua drawing on the same standard, the LXX bridge connecting tamim to teleios
- James 1:4 — _"Let endurance have its perfect (teleios) work, that you may be perfect (teleios) and complete, lacking in nothing"_ (WEB) — the completion sense of teleios reflecting the wholeness of tamim
