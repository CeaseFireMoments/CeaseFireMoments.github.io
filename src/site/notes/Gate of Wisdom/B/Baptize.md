# Define
- Hebrew: 
	- ṭābal or tabal (טָבַל) - To dip, immerse, or plunge something into a liquid (water, blood, dye, etc.). 
		- It carries the sense of submerging an object or person so that the liquid fully covers or adheres to it, often resulting in a change of state (cleansing, dyeing, or consecration).

- Greek:
	- baptizō (βαπτίζω) — to immerse, to dip, to submerge, to make fully wet (used of the ordinance of baptism in the New Testament)


# Related words
- rāḥaṣ (רָחַץ) -  to wash or bathe (often full-body washing
- ṭāhar (טָהַר) -  to be clean or purify (the broader result of ritual washing/immersion)

# Additional Info
- In practice  of (mikveh / ritual immersion), full-body immersion for purification is very close in concept to New Testament baptism, though the specific verb ṭābal is not the most frequent word used for everyday mikveh in the Hebrew Bible. The idea of immersion for cleansing, identification, or consecration is clearly present.
