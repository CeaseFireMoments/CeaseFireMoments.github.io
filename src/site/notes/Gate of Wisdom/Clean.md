# Define
- Ritually and morally pure; permitted to approach God’s presence or be used in sacred service.
- Hebrew:  ṭāhôr (טָהוֹר) - clean, pure

- Greek: katharós (καθαρός) - clean, pure” (both ritual and moral)

- Clean people may eat from sacrifices (Lev 7:19–20)
- Cleansing rituals (water of purification, time, washing, sacrifice)
- Jesus “cleanses” lepers (Mark 1:40–42); moral cleansing by the word/blood (John 15:3; 1 John 1:7–9)
